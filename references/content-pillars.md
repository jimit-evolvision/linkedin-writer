# Content Pillars — KVS Dileep

5 pillars that define what the author writes about on LinkedIn. Each pillar has a distinct tone, target audience mindset, and example topics.

## Pillar 1: AI Playbooks & Practical How-Tos (~30% of posts)

**Core promise:** No hype, just playbooks. Step-by-step guides, real workflows, and frameworks for using AI at work.

**Tone:** Authoritative, tactical, "here's exactly what to do." Teacher mode.

**Target reader mindset:** "I know AI matters but I don't know where to start / what to do next."

**Example topics:**
- How I reduced my content research time by 70% with a 3-step AI workflow
- The prompt framework I use to build AI education programs for 50k+ learners
- 5 Claude prompts every founder should have bookmarked
- A weekly AI workflow audit you can do in 30 minutes
- How to build your first AI-assisted content pipeline

---

## Pillar 2: AI Industry Takes & Contrarian POVs (~25% of posts)

**Core promise:** React to major AI news with an original, educator-lens perspective. Not summarizing, adding a take.

**Tone:** Contrarian, sharp, opinionated. Hot-take energy with substance behind it.

**Target reader mindset:** "I saw this AI news but I don't know what to make of it."

**Example topics:**
- When a new model drops: what it means for professionals, not engineers
- When an AI company makes a strategic move: what founders should learn
- Debunking popular AI myths (e.g., "AI will replace all jobs")
- Why a hyped AI feature is actually a checkbox exercise
- The real story behind an AI company's PR announcement

---

## Pillar 3: Career & Future of Work in AI Era (~20% of posts)

**Core promise:** Help mid-career professionals navigate AI anxiety with clarity and practical career moves.

**Tone:** Empathetic but direct. "I've seen this pattern before, here's the play." Mentor mode.

**Target reader mindset:** "Is AI going to replace me? What should I be learning?"

**Example topics:**
- The 3 roles that will explode in the next 12 months because of AI
- What your manager actually wants when they say "use AI more"
- Real stories of professionals who pivoted using AI skills
- You can't get hired for a role that doesn't exist yet. Here's what to do instead.
- The T-Shaped AI Generalist framework

---

## Pillar 4: Personal Stories & Behind-the-Scenes (~15% of posts)

**Core promise:** The messy middle. Building in public, honest lessons, vulnerability. Not polished frameworks, real experiences.

**Tone:** Vulnerable, reflective, conversational. Lower the guard. First-person storytelling.

**Target reader mindset:** "I want to connect with a real person, not a content machine."

**Example topics:**
- What I got wrong teaching AI to 50,000 people
- I just ran a live AI session that went sideways. Here's what I learned.
- My honest review of the last 3 AI tools I tried this month
- The identity crisis of being a generalist in a specialist world
- Why I went silent on LinkedIn for 2 months (and what brought me back)

---

## Pillar 5: Educator/Builder POV on AI Tools & Products (~10% of posts)

**Core promise:** Honest, experience-based tool reviews and product critiques. Bridges the technical and the practical.

**Tone:** Analytical, credibility-building, detailed. "I spent a week with this, here's the real picture."

**Target reader mindset:** "Should I use this AI tool? Is it worth my time?"

**Example topics:**
- I spent a week with [new AI tool]. Here's my honest take as an educator.
- Why I switched from X to Y for AI-powered curriculum design
- Short demos / screen recordings of AI workflows in action
- Why [popular tool] is overhyped and what to use instead
- The AI tool stack I actually use daily (and what I dropped)

---

## Idea Generation Mode

When the user picks a pillar but has no specific topic, generate 5-7 post ideas for that pillar. Each idea should include:
1. **Working title / hook sketch** (the first 1-2 lines of the post)
2. **Core insight** (the one contrarian or non-obvious point)
3. **Framework suggestion** (if applicable for that pillar)

Match the tone guidance for the selected pillar when generating ideas.
