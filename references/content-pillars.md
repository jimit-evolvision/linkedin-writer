# Content Pillars — Jimit Joshi / AI Access

5 pillars for Jimit's LinkedIn presence as CEO & Founder of Evolvision Technologies, building AI Access. Every post falls under one of these pillars. The campaign goal is to build awareness, establish credibility in AI hiring, and onboard pilot agencies.

---

## Pillar 1: Hiring Pain Points & Industry Reality (~30% of posts)

**Core promise:** Real problems, real numbers. No hype. The waste, cost, and fraud inside high-volume hiring that nobody quantifies.

**Tone:** Direct, grounded, first-person observation. "I watched this happen" energy. Numbers over adjectives.

**Target reader mindset:** "I deal with this every week and nobody has named it this clearly before."

**Example topics:**
- The real cost of phone screening 100 candidates manually (time × recruiter hourly rate)
- Proxy interviews: how common they actually are, and what one bad hire actually costs
- Why high-volume screening is unsolvable at human scale without compromising quality
- The no-show problem: how much budget gets wasted on candidates who ghost after screening
- What an ATS actually does vs what people think it does (it doesn't interview anyone)
- Call fatigue: how screening quality degrades after the 12th candidate call of the day

---

## Pillar 2: AI in Hiring — Contrarian Takes (~25% of posts)

**Core promise:** The hiring tech market is full of noise. Here's what's actually true, from someone who built in this space.

**Tone:** Contrarian, sharp, backed by specifics. "I built this so I know the difference" energy.

**Target reader mindset:** "I've seen all these AI hiring claims. I don't know what to believe."

**Example topics:**
- Why pre-recorded video interviews were never real AI screening (and what adaptive means)
- What HireVue sells vs what Indian agencies actually need
- "AI screening" that's just keyword matching — why it's worse than reading the CV yourself
- Why your ATS is not a screening tool and never was designed to be
- The $25K problem: why global hiring tools price out 90% of Indian agencies
- Real-time vs async: why the follow-up question is everything in screening

---

## Pillar 3: Building AI Access — Founder POV (~20% of posts)

**Core promise:** Transparent, honest building in public. What we decided, why we decided it, what we got wrong.

**Tone:** Vulnerable, direct, first-person. No PR polish. Pre-launch honesty is a feature, not a weakness.

**Target reader mindset:** "I want to follow a builder who tells the truth about the process."

**Example topics:**
- Why I spent 8 months building an AI interviewer (the problem I kept watching happen)
- The hardest product decision: real-time voice vs pre-recorded, and why we chose real-time
- What building an integrity suite taught me about candidate behavior patterns
- Per-minute pricing: why we didn't go subscription-first for the Indian market
- The pilot plan: 10 agencies, free credits, structured feedback — why this matters more than a launch
- What the first version got wrong (and what we rebuilt)

---

## Pillar 4: Technical Hiring Realities (~15% of posts)

**Core promise:** Tactical and practical. How to run technical screening without losing engineers and candidates to tool fragmentation.

**Tone:** Authoritative, technical but accessible. Builder-to-builder credibility. Engineering manager energy.

**Target reader mindset:** "Our technical hiring process is a mess and I know it."

**Example topics:**
- You need 4-6 different tools to hire one backend developer. That's the actual problem.
- Live code tests vs take-home assignments: what actually predicts on-the-job performance
- How to write a technical competency rubric that doesn't just test syntax knowledge
- Why estimation and case problems belong in technical interviews (and how most companies run them badly)
- What a good technical screening report looks like vs what most tools produce

---

## Pillar 5: Indian Hiring Market (~10% of posts)

**Core promise:** India-specific. Not borrowed from Silicon Valley playbooks. Built for the volume and budget reality of Indian agencies.

**Tone:** Informed, market-aware, proud of building for this context specifically.

**Target reader mindset:** "Finally someone building for Indian hiring scale and not just copying a US product."

**Example topics:**
- Why ₹4/min changes who can afford AI screening in India
- The Indian staffing agency reality: 50 openings, 2 recruiters, 400 applications this week
- Why global hiring platforms fail the Indian market (pricing, language, volume expectations)
- The tier-2 city talent pipeline problem that no HR tech product addresses
- India's hiring volume vs India's HR tech investment: the gap that exists and why

## Idea Generation Mode

When the user picks a pillar but has no specific topic, generate 5-7 post ideas for that pillar. Each idea should include:
1. **Working hook** (first 1-2 lines of the post)
2. **Core insight** (the one contrarian or non-obvious point)
3. **Framework suggestion** (if applicable for the pillar)
4. **CTA type** (engagement question, repost prompt, or PILOT comment CTA)

For Pillar 3 (Founder POV) and any pilot-related post, default to the PILOT comment CTA format.
