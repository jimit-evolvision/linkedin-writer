Your recruiter spent 12 hours on screening calls this week.

On paper, that's 48 candidates at 15 minutes each.

Except nobody screens 48 candidates in 12 hours.

The real number is closer to 28. And by candidate 15, the quality of decisions is already slipping.

Here's where the time actually goes:

The Screening Hour Stack

1. Scheduling drag: Finding a slot, sending a link, chasing WhatsApp replies. 5-8 minutes per candidate before the call even starts.
2. No-show tax: 20-30% of candidates don't show. You wait, follow up, or mark the slot wasted.
3. The call itself: 15 minutes when it goes cleanly. 25 when the candidate runs long or the connection drops.
4. Documentation lag: 5-10 minutes of notes while memory is fresh. Skip this and you're shortlisting on gut, not data.
5. Fatigue multiplier: After 10-12 back-to-back calls, the bar for "good enough" quietly drops. You stop probing.

Real time per screened candidate: 35-45 minutes. Not 15.

For an agency screening 60-80 candidates/month, that's 35-60 hours of recruiter time on screening alone. At market rates, that's ₹10,000-17,000 before a single shortlist reaches the client.

That cost is on the timesheet.

The quality problem isn't.

The first 10 screens in a session produce sharp calls. The last 10 produce noise. Your client receives a mix of both in the same shortlist.
