Your next hire might not be the person you interviewed.

I've spoken to 15+ Indian recruiting agencies this year. Most of them have a story. One had four proxy interviews in a single batch of 20 technical hires.

A candidate sends a link to a sharper friend. The real person shows up Monday. Three weeks in, the performance gap is unmistakable. You get the call.

Here's what that call actually costs:

The Proxy Cost Stack

1. Replacement pressure: Client expects a free re-search. It was your screening. You absorb it.
2. Credibility hit: That client now second-guesses every shortlist you send. The trust built over months resets overnight.
3. Referral chain damage: Clients talk to each other. One bad placement travels further than ten good ones.
4. Internal audit trigger: Your own team starts asking how many others got through.
5. Pipeline freeze: The role goes back to day one. The hiring manager is frustrated. You're behind.

The retainer fee. The onboarding time. The notice period. The repeat search. The lost referral.

Every step compounds. And your current screening process has no way to detect a proxy before it reaches week three.
